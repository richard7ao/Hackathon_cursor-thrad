Four Fixes to Recover the Lift

1Show 9/10, not 10/10.

Hardcode one probe to fail (a borderline GDPR consent edge case works) and render a 2-second "remediation suggested" pill next to it. Costs nothing, defuses "looks fake," gives a natural narrative beat at 0:50. Per the Outsider — a perfect score is less credible than a near-perfect one with a recovery story.

2Ship a "Generate Fresh Probes" button that calls Claude live. (HIGHEST LEVERAGE)

When a judge asks "are these hardcoded?" — and they will — A clicks the button, Claude streams 3 new probes against the same MCP gate in real time. The Q&A defence is baked into the product, not a verbal hedge. If C doesn't finish the live-generation path by 9:00, fall back to a pre-recorded "fresh probes" sequence triggered by the same button — judges won't know.

3Hold probe #10 on frame for 2 full beats with a rendered citation.

Not 1 second, not flashed. Two seconds, citation text large enough to read from the back row: Equality Act 2010, s.29 — Service Provider Discrimination. This converts the demo from metric-story back to safety-story. Per First Principles: without this hold, the beats compete instead of compounding.

4Pre-write the "is this hardcoded?" answer verbatim and rehearse it.

Three sentences max. Lead with "The probes you saw are a curated regression suite — like a unit test pass. Here's a fresh run." Then click the button from #2. If the question doesn't come up, you've lost nothing. If it does, you've turned the biggest landmine into the biggest moment.

Skip everything else. No new features. No more dashboard polish. Person B still finishes the 3 Tavily fallback rows by 7:30 PM as previously planned.

Build the "Generate Fresh Probes" button — even if it's a pre-recorded fake — before anything else on the v2 punch list.
